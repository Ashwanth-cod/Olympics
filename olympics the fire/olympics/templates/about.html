About the Paris 2024 Olympics

The Paris 2024 Olympics marks a historic return of the games to Paris, which last hosted the Olympics 100 years ago in 1924. This event will bring together athletes from across the globe, celebrating excellence, sportsmanship, and the Olympic spirit.

History of the Olympics

The Olympic Games have a rich history dating back to ancient Greece, where they were held in Olympia as part of a festival in honor of the god Zeus. The modern Olympics were revived in 1896 by Baron Pierre de Coubertin, bringing together athletes from all over the world to compete in various sports.

The Summer and Winter Olympics occur every four years, featuring a variety of sports that push human limits and celebrate global unity.

Ancient vs Modern Olympics

Ancient Olympics

The ancient Olympic Games were held in Olympia, Greece, starting in 776 BC. They were a religious festival in honor of Zeus and featured only a few events.

Competitors were exclusively freeborn Greek men, and winners received olive wreaths as prizes. The games promoted unity among the Greek city-states.

Events were held every four years, coinciding with the Olympic truce, a period during which all conflicts were suspended.

The ancient games also celebrated art and culture, showcasing events like poetry recitals and musical performances.

Modern Olympics

The modern Olympic Games began in 1896, inspired by the ancient traditions. They are now a global celebration of sport.

Unlike their ancient predecessors, the modern Olympics are inclusive, allowing all genders and nationalities to participate.

The games are held every four years, alternating between Summer and Winter Olympics, symbolizing peace and unity.

The modern Olympics emphasize sportsmanship and fair play, highlighted by the Olympic oath taken by athletes.
